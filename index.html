Doctoral Candidate in Bioinformatics

2019 - 2022, Mohammed V University, Rabat, Morocco

Ph.D. Research Contributions

During my Ph.D. research, I made significant contributions to understanding the evolution of SARS-CoV-2 and its genetic variations, particularly during the global COVID-19 pandemic. My work emphasized the development of computational tools and databases to monitor and analyze large-scale viral genomic data. Some key achievements include:

  • Development of Bioinformatics Tools: I developed Bingo-3C, an innovative in-house software for DNA sequence compression, comparison, and clustering. This tool efficiently minimized the time and memory needs for handling vast quantities of DNA sequence data by introducing a novel representation method. It proved essential in tracking the genetic changes in SARS-CoV-2 sequences.
  • Creation of IDbSV Database: I designed and managed the IDbSV database, a comprehensive repository of over 60,000 genetic alterations from more than a million SARS-CoV-2 genomes. This database, publicly available since November 2020, enabled researchers globally to understand the sources and impacts of mutations in real-time, playing a crucial role in pandemic response efforts.
  • Analysis of SARS-CoV-2 Evolution: My thesis presented a detailed analysis of the evolution of SARS-CoV-2 over four years (2020-2023), summarizing key findings on mutation hotspots, geographical spread, and temporal trends. I monitored and characterized the virus’s mutation rate and evolutionary patterns, contributing to global efforts in tracking its spread and assisting in the design of public health strategies.
  • Impact of the Work: The bioinformatics tools and databases developed during this period were instrumental in processing millions of genomes and summarizing crucial findings that aided in the international scientific community's understanding of the virus.
